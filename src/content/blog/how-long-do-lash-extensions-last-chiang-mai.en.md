# How Long Do Lash Extensions Last? An Honest Answer from a Chiang Mai Lash Artist

![Close-up of natural-look lash extensions by My Lash House Chiang Mai — illustrating typical lash extension longevity and aftercare](/images/service-natural.jpg)

**Short answer: with proper care, a professionally applied set of lash extensions lasts three to four weeks before you'll want a refill.** Individual extensions shed naturally with the lashes they're glued to — every natural lash sheds and regrows on a 60–90 day cycle, and the extension comes with it.

That's the honest answer. The longer version explains why some clients get four full weeks and others lose half the set in the first week — and what you can actually control.

## What "lasting" really means

A lash extension doesn't fall out on its own. It's glued to one natural lash, and it falls when that lash falls.

You shed between one and five natural lashes per day, every day, with or without extensions. By week two, you'll have lost 20–40% of your set just from your normal lash cycle. By week three, the set looks noticeably gappier. By week four, most clients want a refill or a fresh full set.

That's the natural baseline. Everything below either holds you to that baseline — or shortens it.

## What actually affects how long they last

Five things matter, in roughly this order of impact.

### 1. Glue quality

Cheap lash glue breaks down with heat and humidity. Chiang Mai in the rainy season is a stress test for any adhesive that wasn't made for warm climates. Premium medical-grade glue holds against sweat, steam, and the daily moisture in the air.

If your last set fell apart in the first week and you live somewhere humid, the glue is the most likely culprit.

### 2. Application technique

Handmade fans are lighter and balanced. Each fan is built one lash at a time, with the curl and spread shaped for the specific natural lash it's going on. They sit cleanly and shed naturally.

Premade fans are heavier and stiffer. They're faster for the artist, but the extra weight on a single natural lash pulls it loose sooner. The cheaper the salon, the more likely they're using premades.

Isolation matters just as much as the fan. If two natural lashes get glued together (a common shortcut when an artist is rushing), they pull each other out the moment one of them starts to shed. You can lose four extensions in one go from one bad isolation.

### 3. Your aftercare

This is the part you control. See the next section.

### 4. Your skin type

Oily skin breaks down lash adhesive faster than dry skin. If you naturally have oily eyelids, or you use rich creams and oils around the eye area, extensions shed quicker. You can stretch a set a bit longer by using oil-free cleansers and being more careful at the lash line.

### 5. Your natural lashes

Damaged, brittle, or very fine natural lashes hold less weight and shed faster. If you've been wearing extensions back-to-back for years, your natural lashes may be in a recovery phase. A break (or moving to a lighter style like Classic 1:1 or Light Volume) often gives you a longer-lasting set on the next round.

## Why some shops' lashes only last a week

The honest answers, in order:

- **Premade fans on every client.** Heavier, fall out faster.
- **Cheap or expired glue.** Especially in humid climates.
- **Rushed isolation.** Clusters of glued-together lashes come out as a clump.
- **Wrong curl or weight for your lashes.** A heavy D-curl Mega set on weak natural lashes will shed in days.
- **No aftercare consultation.** You leave not knowing you can't get them wet for 24 hours.

A good lash artist takes longer per appointment, costs more, and lasts longer per set. The math usually works out in favour of the better salon, even before you factor in the look.

## Aftercare — what you control

These are the rules that actually move the dial. None are unreasonable.

**First 24 hours.** No water on the lashes. No steam. That means no hot showers in the face, no swimming, no hot yoga, no sauna. The glue takes a full day to cure properly. Get them wet too soon and the bonds set weakly.

**First week.** Avoid oil-based skincare and makeup near the eye area. Oil dissolves lash adhesive. Read your cleanser, eye cream, and remover labels — "oil-free" or "water-based" is what you want.

**Every day, ongoing:**

- Clean the lash line with a dedicated lash shampoo (foam-type works best). Once a day is plenty.
- Brush the lashes gently with the spoolie we send you home with, especially after sleep.
- Pat dry with a lint-free cloth — no rubbing, no cotton pads.

**Sleeping.** A silk pillowcase reduces friction. Sleeping on your back is gentler on the set than face-down. If you're a stomach sleeper, even silk helps.

**Things to avoid completely:**

- Rubbing your eyes.
- Mascara on the extensions.
- Eyelash curlers.
- Oil-based makeup removers.
- Pulling at the extensions when they start to feel loose. Let them shed naturally.

Follow this list and you'll get the full 3–4 weeks. Ignore it and you'll halve that, regardless of how well the set was applied.

## When to book your refill

The sweet spot is between **two and three weeks** after your full set. The set still looks dense enough to be flattering, and the artist has enough remaining lashes to map cleanly into.

Signs you've waited too long:

- More than 50% of the set has shed.
- The remaining extensions are growing out at odd angles.
- You can see significant gaps along the lash line.

If it's been longer than four weeks, a refill is often more work than a fresh full set — and looks worse for the price. Better to book a new full set and reset the cycle.

Refill pricing depends on the style you originally had and how much of it remains. We confirm the price at the start of every refill appointment, after a quick look at how the set's aged.

## Common questions about longevity

A few more things first-time clients ask in the chair.

**Can I make a set last six weeks?** Realistically, no. Even with perfect aftercare, your natural lash cycle is what it is. By week six you've shed most of the extensions through normal regrowth.

**Why did my last shop's lashes only last a week?** Almost always glue, application, or aftercare. See the sections above.

**Do refills last as long as a fresh set?** Yes, when done at the right time (week 2–3). The new fans last 3–4 weeks from the refill date, just like a full set.

**Will my natural lashes recover if I take a break?** Yes. The lash cycle is 60–90 days. After a single full cycle without extensions, your natural lashes are back to baseline.
