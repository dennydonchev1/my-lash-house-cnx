## Common questions about lash extensions in Chiang Mai

### How much do lash extensions cost in Chiang Mai?

Full-set lash extension pricing in Chiang Mai ranges roughly ฿590 to ฿3,000+ depending on style and studio. My Lash House publishes ฿590–฿1,590 across the full menu. Somsasi, Eye to Eye Nimman, and Lash Berries don't publish pricing in open search; quotes are shared on request via DM or booking platform. Infill appointments (every 2–4 weeks) typically cost 50–70% of the original set. If pricing is the priority, ask explicitly whether fans are handmade or premade before booking — handmade-fan technique is one of the strongest predictors of retention and natural-lash health.

### How long do lash extensions last?

A well-applied lash set lasts 2–4 weeks before infill, with the natural shedding cycle of your real lashes setting the upper bound. You shed 2–5 natural lashes per day; an extension goes with each one. Heavier styles (mega-volume) shed faster than lighter styles (classic) because of the extra weight. Side-sleepers and frequent water-exposers shed faster too. With My Lash House's 3-day retouch guarantee, unusual early shedding caused by glue or application issues on the studio's side is covered. Somsasi offers a 7-day extension warranty. Beyond those windows, schedule infills every 2–4 weeks to maintain a full look.

### Are lash extensions safe?

Lash extensions are safe when applied by a careful artist using fans matched to the strength of your natural lashes. Risks come from rushed isolation (clusters of natural lashes glued together fall out together and damage real lashes), fans that are too heavy for the natural lash, allergic reactions to the cyanoacrylate adhesive (rare but possible), and improper aftercare. Tell your artist about any sensitivities, ask whether they isolate one lash at a time, and ask about a patch test if you're worried. The single biggest safety factor is choosing an artist who isolates carefully, not choosing the cheapest salon.

### What's the difference between classic, hybrid, volume, and mega-volume lash extensions?

- **Classic:** one extension per natural lash. Subtle, mascara-like effect. Lightest. Fastest application. Best for first-timers and natural looks.
- **Hybrid:** mix of classic single extensions and lightweight handmade fans. More texture than classic, less drama than volume. The everyday-glamour middle ground.
- **Volume:** handmade fans (3–6 thin extensions per fan) attached to one natural lash. Full, fluffy, photogenic. Heavier than classic.
- **Mega-volume:** larger handmade fans (10–16 extensions per fan). Most dramatic. Heaviest. Requires strong natural lashes and careful application. Not for everyone.

If you're new to extensions, start classic or hybrid. Save mega-volume for clients with strong natural lashes who specifically want the look.

### Are lash extensions worth it?

Lash extensions don't suit everyone. Decide whether they fit your routine before choosing a salon.

**Reasons to get lash extensions:**

- **Time savings.** No daily mascara, no eyelash curler, fewer steps in the morning routine.
- **Look-good factor.** Wider, fuller, more open-eyed appearance with no makeup.
- **Photo-ready.** Especially helpful for travelers, brides, or anyone photographed often.
- **Confidence.** Many clients report a noticeable confidence lift in the first few days.
- **No mascara smudging.** Especially valuable in Thailand's heat and humidity.

**Reasons to think twice:**

- **Cost over time.** Extensions need infill appointments every 2–4 weeks. Annual cost adds up. Set a budget you're comfortable with before starting.
- **Time commitment.** Initial sets take 90–180 minutes. Infills take 60–90 minutes.
- **Lifestyle adjustments.** Avoid oil-based cleansers and heavy water exposure for the first 24–48 hours. Side-sleepers may experience faster shedding.
- **Risk if done poorly.** Bad application can damage natural lashes, especially if isolation is rushed or fans are too heavy. Choosing a careful artist matters more than choosing a cheap one.
- **Allergic reactions.** Reactions to lash adhesive happen to a small percentage of clients. Tell your artist about any sensitivities before your first set, and ask about a patch test if you're worried.

The honest take: lash extensions are worth it if you can budget the upkeep (financial and lifestyle) and choose an artist who isolates carefully and uses fans matched to your natural lash strength. They aren't worth it if you'd resent the maintenance schedule or the cost. There's no shame in deciding lashes aren't for you. A lash lift, mascara, or no makeup at all are all fine options.

## What should I ask before booking lash extensions?

Before committing to any lash artist in Chiang Mai, ask these three questions:

**Are the fans handmade or premade?** Handmade fans are sized to the specific natural lash. Premade fans are heavier and shed faster, especially on weak or thin natural lashes. If a salon uses premade fans, the price should be cheaper. That's the trade-off.

**Is isolation done one lash at a time?** Proper application isolates each natural lash before applying an extension. If clusters of natural lashes get glued together (because the artist rushed isolation), they fall out together and can damage the real lashes.

**What's the retouch / damage policy?** Some salons offer a refund or free retouch within a certain window if a set sheds unusually fast. If a salon doesn't have any policy at all, that's a quality signal worth weighing.

If a salon can't or won't answer these questions clearly, that itself is information.

**What's the difference between handmade and premade lash fans?**

Handmade fans are built by the lash artist during the appointment, sized to each individual natural lash. Premade fans come pre-clustered from suppliers and are heavier, less customizable, and shed faster on weak or thin natural lashes. The trade-off is price: premade fans are cheaper to apply, but retention and natural-lash health both suffer.

**Will lash extensions damage my natural lashes?**

Properly applied extensions don't damage natural lashes. Damage usually comes from oversized fans on weak lashes, poor isolation that lets two natural lashes glue together, or rough at-home removal. The 0.05mm handmade fans used at My Lash House are isolated one natural lash at a time and shed naturally with the lash they're attached to.

**Can I wear mascara with lash extensions?**

Generally no. Mascara shortens retention significantly and is hard to remove without damaging the bond. The extensions themselves provide the mascara effect, so mascara is unnecessary.
