So you just got your lashes done. Now what?

The short version: keep them dry for 24 hours, stay away from oil-based anything near your eyes, and brush them daily with a clean spoolie. Do that, and your set lasts a full 3 to 4 weeks. Skip it, and you're booking a refill in week 2 wondering what happened.

This is the practical day-by-day handbook. Real rules, why they matter, and which ones you can relax on once you know what you're doing.

## What's the quickest aftercare summary?

Three things move the needle on retention, and only three.

**One**, keep the lashes dry for 24 hours so the cyanoacrylate adhesive can fully cure.

**Two**, skip oil-based skincare and makeup near your eye area indefinitely. Oil dissolves the adhesive bond, every time.

**Three**, clean daily with a foam lash shampoo and brush through with your spoolie.

Everything else (sleep position, mascara, eyelash curlers, refill cadence) matters at the margins. Nail the big three and a properly applied set lasts the full 3 to 4 weeks. Skip them and the set halves in lifespan no matter how good your artist is.

> 💡 If you only remember one thing from this guide, make it the oil rule. Skincare with even a little oil near your lash line will quietly shorten retention more than almost anything else you do or skip.

## What do I do in the first 24 hours after my appointment?

This is the curing window, and it's the one rule you absolutely cannot bend.

The cyanoacrylate adhesive used in lash extensions takes a full day to reach maximum bond strength. Water exposure during this window weakens those bonds permanently. There's no fixing it after the fact.

> ⚠️ No showers with water on your face, no swimming, no steam, no heavy sweating, no makeup near your eyes, and no sleeping face-down. For the first 24 hours, this part really isn't optional.

### What to avoid for the first 24 hours

- Showers with water directly on your face
- Swimming, hot springs, the ocean
- Steam (hot yoga, sauna, steam room, even opening the dishwasher)
- Sweat from heavy exercise
- Makeup of any kind near the eye
- Crying (whenever possible, you know how it goes)
- Sleeping face-down

### What's totally fine

- Drinking water normally
- Washing your hands
- Eating normally (just don't lean over a steaming bowl of soup)
- Light walking and movement
- Working at your screen

If you absolutely have to exercise in the first 24 hours, keep your heart rate low enough that sweat doesn't form. A short walk is fine. A hot yoga class is a no.

## What about the first week?

After the 24-hour cure, your daily-life rules kick in. Most retention slip-ups in the first week come down to three usual suspects: oil-based skincare, makeup remover wipes, and waterproof mascara residue.

### Which skincare products should I retire from my eye-area rotation?

- Oil cleansers
- Micellar water (most formulas contain solvents that dissolve lash adhesive)
- Cleansing balms
- Eye creams with retinol or rich oil bases
- Makeup remover wipes (almost all use oil-based formulas to dissolve waterproof products)

### Which skincare products are safe near my eyes?

- Water-based foaming cleansers
- Gel cleansers labeled "oil-free"
- Lightweight, water-based moisturizers (applied below your brow bone, not at your lash line)

### Can I still wear makeup with lash extensions?

Mascara isn't necessary anymore. Your extensions already do the mascara thing for you (fuller, darker, more defined), so skip it.

If you genuinely need mascara for a specific event, use only water-based, lash-extension-safe formulas. Apply to the tips of your extensions, never the roots.

Eyeshadow is fine. Powder formulas are safest. Cream shadows with oil bases shorten retention.

Eyeliner against your lash line is fine. Oil-based liner pencils dissolve adhesive, so skip those.

## How do I clean lash extensions daily?

Two small habits separate a set that ages beautifully from one that looks ragged by week two.

### How often should I clean them?

Foam-type lash shampoo is the standard. Apply with the soft brush we send you home with, massage gently along your lash line (not just the tips), rinse with cool water, pat dry with a lint-free cloth.

Skip cotton pads. They catch on extensions and pull them out.

> 💡 Here's the counterintuitive part: clean lashes last longer than dirty ones. Built-up skin oils, makeup residue, and dust slowly weaken the adhesive bond. Daily cleaning prevents that buildup.

### How do I use the spoolie?

That spoolie we sent you home with? That's its job.

Gentle upward strokes from base to tip. Separate any extensions that crossed during sleep. Twenty seconds, max. That's the whole routine.

### Should I pat or rub when drying?

After cleansing, after the shower, after a rainy walk to the car. Always pat with a lint-free cloth.

Rubbing is the single most damaging daily habit. It breaks the adhesive bond and pulls your natural lashes out at the root. Be gentle.

## What should I never do with lash extensions?

A short list of things that, in 7+ years on the chair, consistently halve a set's lifespan.

- **Oil-based cleansers, removers, and makeup wipes** (the single biggest retention killer)
- **Waterproof mascara** (requires oil-based remover, which dissolves adhesive)
- **Eyelash curlers** (bend the extensions out of shape and crack the bond)
- **Rubbing your eyes**, especially when you're sleepy or itchy
- **Picking or pulling at loose extensions** (let them shed naturally)
- **Sauna, steam room, hot yoga in the first 48 hours** (moisture plus heat weakens fresh bonds)
- **Sleeping face-down on your lashes for hours**

The sauna rule relaxes after the first 48 hours. After that, occasional steam exposure is fine. Just rinse and pat dry afterward.

## How should I sleep without losing lashes?

Sleep position matters more than most clients expect.

A pillow pressing against your lashes for 6 to 8 hours a night slowly breaks down the bonds. The side you favor takes the hit first.

**Back sleepers** retain best. By a wide margin.

**Side sleepers** lose lashes on the side they sleep on faster than the opposite side. By week 3, the asymmetry is visible.

**Stomach sleepers** lose lashes the fastest of all. If switching positions isn't realistic, a silk pillowcase reduces friction enough to help.

> ✅ A silk pillowcase is a worthwhile investment for anyone who can't sleep on their back. Less friction. Doesn't absorb skin oils the way cotton does. Extends retention by an estimated 20 to 30%. Plus, your hair will thank you.

## When should I come in for a refill?

Most clients refill every 2 to 3 weeks.

Lighter styles (Classic 1:1, Light Volume) hold a bit longer and can stretch to 3 to 4 weeks. Heavier styles (Russian Volume, Mega Volume) need 2 to 3 weeks to look full.

The sweet spot for a refill is when your set has retained roughly 40 to 60% of the original extensions. Enough remains for Ying to map cleanly into the existing shape. Not so few that the appointment becomes a near-rebuild.

### How do I know I waited too long?

- More than 50% of the set has shed
- Visible gaps along your lash line
- Remaining extensions are growing out at angles that don't sit cleanly
- The set no longer looks like the original design

If it's been 4+ weeks, a refill often costs about the same as a fresh full set and looks worse afterward. Start fresh instead.
